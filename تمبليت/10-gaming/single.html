GTA VI Review: Rockstar Redefines Open World Gaming in a Masterpiece Decade in the Making

After 12 years of development and the most anticipated game launch in history, Grand Theft Auto VI doesn't just meet expectations — it obliterates them.

Kyle Mitchell
Senior Games Editor
June 15, 2026 12 min read 2.4M views
PlatformPS6 / Xbox Series X / PC
DeveloperRockstar Games
GenreOpen World / Action
Price$79.99
Play Time~80 hours story

Let's cut straight to it: Grand Theft Auto VI is the greatest open world game ever made. It's not a matter of personal preference or nostalgia goggles. In virtually every measurable dimension — world design, story, characters, physics, NPC AI, mission variety, and sheer scale — Rockstar has produced something that will define the medium for the next decade.

The World: Vice City Like You've Never Seen

Returning to Vice City felt like revisiting a dream. But this is Vice City transformed — a living, breathing metropolitan area spanning 4x the size of Los Santos in GTAV, populated with NPCs that have daily routines, remember player actions, and respond dynamically to everything from weather to the player's reputation.

"GTA VI is not a game. It's a destination. I genuinely lost track of time just driving around, watching the city exist." — Kyle Mitchell, LevelUp

Story: Two Protagonists, One Masterpiece

Playing as both Lucia and Jason, GTA VI tells the most sophisticated story in the franchise's history — a love story wrapped in crime, trauma, and the American Dream's hollow promise. The performances are Oscar-worthy, and the tonal shifts between comedy, tragedy, and action are expertly handled.

Gameplay: Everything Works

The gunplay has been completely rebuilt. Cars handle with precision. The new wanted system creates genuinely tense police encounters. And for the first time in the franchise, stealth missions actually work.
