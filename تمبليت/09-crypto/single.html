Bitcoin Breaks $107,000: On-Chain Data Points to $120K Target as Institutional Wave Intensifies

BTC hit a new all-time high as ETF inflows reach $3.2B in a single week — the most since January 2024. Analysts are now revising year-end targets upward.

Michael Torres
Bitcoin Analyst
June 15, 2026 8 min read 186,000 views

Bitcoin crossed the $107,000 mark early Monday morning, reaching a new all-time high and extending the 2026 bull market cycle. The move comes after a 72-hour consolidation period above $104,000, during which on-chain metrics continued to flash bullish signals.

What's Driving the Move?

Three key factors are pushing Bitcoin to new highs:

  • ETF Inflows: US spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded $3.2B in net inflows last week, the highest since January 2024. BlackRock's IBIT alone attracted $1.8B.
  • Halving Effect: The April 2024 halving's supply shock is now fully priced in, with exchange BTC reserves at a 7-year low.
  • Corporate Treasury: MicroStrategy, now holding 570,000 BTC, has been joined by 28 more public companies adding BTC to their treasury in Q2 2026.
"This is unlike any previous cycle. Institutional investors are absorbing nearly 3x the daily issuance from miners. The supply crisis is real." — PlanB, Bitcoin Analyst

Technical Analysis

From a technical perspective, Bitcoin has broken out of the $100K–$106K range with significant volume. The next major resistance levels are at $112,000 (1.618 Fibonacci extension) and $120,000 (psychological level with options clusters).

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) on the weekly chart stands at 72, indicating bullish momentum without extreme overbought conditions. The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) shows a fresh bullish crossover on the daily chart.

What Could Stop the Rally?

Risks include a sudden regulatory shock (SEC enforcement action), broader risk-off sentiment in equities, or a Mt. Gox-related distribution event. However, current on-chain data suggests large holders are accumulating, not distributing.
