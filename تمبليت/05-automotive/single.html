Tesla Model S Plaid 2 Review: The Most Insane Electric Car Ever Built

700-mile EPA range. 1,020 horsepower. 0-60mph in 1.7 seconds. Tesla's flagship isn't just a car — it's a statement about what's possible.

Senior Road Test Editor

Overview

Tesla has done it again. The Model S Plaid 2 isn't just an update — it's a complete reimagining of what a luxury electric sedan can be. Where the original Plaid shocked the world with its 2.0-second 0-60 time, the Plaid 2 goes further with a new tri-motor setup producing 1,020 horsepower and a new 120kWh solid-state battery that delivers an EPA-rated 700 miles of range.

Performance

The 1.7-second 0-60mph figure sounds like a typo, but it's very real and deeply unsettling. The sensation of full power acceleration in the Plaid 2 is less like being in a car and more like being briefly confused about which direction is forward. It's genuinely a transformative driving experience.

"Every car I drive after the Plaid 2 feels like it's missing something. That something is physics-bending performance." — James Wheeler, AutoDrive

Range & Charging

The 700-mile EPA range number is real-world achievable. In our testing at highway speeds (75mph), we averaged 640 miles per charge. When connected to Tesla's new V5 Supercharger network, the car charges from 10% to 80% in just 15 minutes — a remarkable achievement.

✓ Pros

  • Unprecedented 700-mile range
  • Mind-bending acceleration
  • Stunning 18" center display
  • Supercharger network advantage
  • Over-the-air software updates
  • Autopilot FSD included

✗ Cons

  • $124,990 starting price is steep
  • Rear legroom still compromised
  • Yoke steering divisive
  • Limited dealer support

Verdict

★★★★★

9.4/10 — Excellent. The Tesla Model S Plaid 2 is the best production car available today by several measurable metrics. If you can afford it, nothing else comes close.
