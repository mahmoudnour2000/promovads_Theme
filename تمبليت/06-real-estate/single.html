Record-Breaking Performance

Dubai's real estate market reached an unprecedented milestone in Q2 2026, recording AED 30 billion ($8.2 billion) in transactions — a 24% increase over Q2 2025 and a new all-time high for a single quarter. The number of individual transactions, at 34,280, also set a new record.

What's Driving the Boom?

Three factors are accelerating Dubai's property market: continued influx of high-net-worth individuals from Russia, Europe, and Asia; the strength of UAE's economy relative to global uncertainty; and the massive off-plan development pipeline offering attractive payment plans.

"Dubai has permanently shifted from a cyclical market to a structural growth story. The fundamentals have never been stronger." — James Allsopp, CBRE MENA

Hot Neighborhoods

Palm Jumeirah (+41% YoY), Business Bay (+28%), and the new Emaar Beachfront development (+58%) led price growth. Off-plan launches in Sobha Hartland 2 and Damac Hills 3 sold out within 48 hours of launch.
